Order of Operations.

Rules which express what operation to perform first.

Definitions.

Operator: A mathematical symbol which is used to represent some behavior/effect. For example '\(+\)' & '\(-\)' are operators since they represent addition and subtraction respectively.

Operand: Any number or variable.

Variable: A letter which is used to represent some unknown value.

E.g

a) \(x + 7 = 4\),

Here there are 3 Operands, and 1 Operator.

Operands: \(x\), \(7\), \(4\)
Operator: \(+\)

Key Concept.

The order of operations are a set of rules that inform which mathematical operation to perform first. Luckily, there is an easy way to remember the order of operations — BEDMAS.

Brackets
Exponents
Division
Multiplication
Addition
Subtraction

Important Note!

Keep in mind that;

  • Multiplication and Division have equal priority
  • Addition and Subtraction have equal priority

Solved Examples.

1. Solve the following questions

a) \( 24 ÷ (2 + 10) = ? \) b) \( (3 + 5) × 2 + 3^2= ? \)

1a) Recall, we must follow BEDMAS!

We start inside the brackets by adding \(2 + 10 = 12\)

Finally, we divide \(24 ÷ 12 = 2\)

E.g

\(= 24 ÷ (2 + 10) \)
\(= 24 ÷ 12 \)
\(= 2 \)

1b) Recall, we must follow BEDMAS!

We start inside the brackets by adding \( 3 + 5 = 8\)

Next, we go to the exponents so \( 3^2 = 3 × 3 = 9 \)

After, we multiply \( 8 × 2 = 16 \)

Finally, we add \( 16 + 9 = 25 \)

E.g

\(= (3 + 5) × 2 + 3^2 \)
\(= (8) × 2 + 3^2 \)
\(= 8 × 2 + 9 \)
\(= 16 + 9 \)
\(= 25 \)

Practice Questions.

a) \( (5 - 2) × 3 = ?\) b) \( (18 - 3^2) × 4 = ?\) c) \( (7 + 3) ÷ (4^2 + 4) = ?\) d) \(6 × 5 ÷ 3 + 2 = ?\)
